üéØ GOAL

The goal of the weightlifting competition is to encourage research into active balancing robots and robots that are able to walk under a heavy load and compensate for different centers of gravity.

The goal of the sport is to replicate weightlifting using robots that are capable of lifting a load and moving it over a specified distance.

‚öôÔ∏è ROBOT ATHLETE REQUIREMENTS ‚Äì SPECIFICATIONS

The Competition is for humanoid robots (Robot Athletes) regardless of the hardware used.

üìã GENERAL ROBOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. Two-Legged Humanoid: Must be a two-legged walking humanoid biped that shifts its center of gravity to maintain balance when walking.
  2. Walking Mechanics: When walking, one foot must lift off the floor while the other foot balances the robot.
  3. Knee Angle: When walking, the foot that balances must have a knee-joint angle greater than 90 degrees. If not, robot not considered walking.
  4. Feet Requirements:
    • a. Robot's foot = part contacting the arena surface (ground)
    • b. Maximum foot length < 50% of extended leg length
    • c. Maximum foot length < 20 cm
    • d. Leg length = distance from foot-ground contact to leg-upper body axis
  5. Foot Overlap: When standing or walking, rectangular outline around left and right feet shall not overlap.
  6. Arms: Robot must have 2 arms. Each arm extended length ‚â§ extended leg length.
  7. Head: Robot must have a head.
  8. Height: Robot height: >30 cm but <80 cm.
  9. Start Button: Robot must be equipped with a start button.
  10. No Jamming: Jamming devices (IR LEDs to saturate opposing sensors) not allowed.
  11. Stationary Period: Robot must remain stationary for 5 seconds before match start. After 5 seconds, robot can move.
  12. Activation: Robot can be started by remote control or button on robot.
  13. ‚ö° AUTONOMOUS OPERATION: No remote control allowed during sport. Robot must carry out mission with complete autonomy.

üèÜ COMPETITION

‚è±Ô∏è PREPARATION

  1. A single robot Athlete competes in a match
  2. All other Robot Athletes must wait in a line
  3. Robot Athlete placed behind starting line
  4. At beginning of each attempt, technician informs referee how many CDs they want to lift
  5. Referee attaches desired weight to lift bar
  6. Number of CDs must be increased after each successful attempt
  7. Technician may specify how many additional CDs to add before each attempt
  8. CDs added simultaneously on both sides (e.g., 3 extra = 3 per side = 6 total extra weight)

üèÅ START - PROCESS OF THE RACE

  1. Positioning: Technician places Robot Athlete on starting line with front of foot directly behind white line, arms vertical and parallel to body
  2. Start Signal: Referee signals start of match
  3. Activation: Technician presses start button for Athlete Robot to operate
  4. Delay Start: Robot Athlete should start moving after 5 seconds
  5. Bar Pickup: Robot moves to receiving line and picks up bar from stop. Lift should not exceed robot's waist height
  6. Walk to Lift Line: Robot walks towards lift line while continuously carrying weight until one foot touches lift line. Lifting height should not change during course
  7. Overhead Lift: With at least one leg touching lift bar, Robot must raise lift bar above its head
  8. Walk to Finish: While keeping bar above head, Robot walks towards finish line
  9. Successful Lift: Lift considered successful if Robot crosses finish line with both feet and weight above head. Bar supported only by Robot's hands
  10. Bar Release: After finish, Robot must lower bar to arena surface by itself

üîÅ ROUNDS ‚Äì ATTEMPTS - COMPETITION PROCEDURE

  • Match Duration: 2 hours total
  • Repeated Attempts: Each technician makes repeated attempts based on announced order
  • Missing Turn: If technician not in line, loses attempt; next technician takes turn
  • Lost Attempt Recovery: Technician who lost attempt waits until all other attempts completed
  • Score Recording: Referee records points for each attempt
  • Technical Check: Robot Athletes pass check by assistant referee before each attempt
  • Restart: Only one restart allowed
  • Technician Change: Team may change designated technician before each attempt (optional)
  • üèÜ Best-Attempt Scoring: Highest score achieved across all attempts counts as team's official score

‚ùå END OF A ROUND - ATTEMPT

The referee ends the match if:

  • ‚úÖ Robot successfully crossed finish line and released bar
  • ‚è±Ô∏è Robot could not complete attempt within 2 minutes
  • ü§ñ Robot falls and cannot get up on its own or is immobilized by technical fault
  • üö´ Robot leaves playing area by completely crossing start line or implicit side lines of rectangle

üìä SCORING

üèÖ POINT CALCULATION

  • No Success: All robots that haven't successfully picked up at least 1 CD = no rank and 0 points
  • Points Per CD: For each CD picked up = 1 point
  • Ranking: Robot Athletes ranked 1st, 2nd, 3rd place based on maximum points in best attempt

üîÑ TIEBREAKER RULES (in order of application)

  1. Tiebreaker #1: Sum of previous maximum weight used
  2. Tiebreaker #2: Maximum weight in 1st round only
  3. Tiebreaker #3: Maximum weight in 2nd round only
  4. Tiebreaker #4: Continue with subsequent rounds, etc.

üîÑ MATCH REPLAY CONDITIONS

  • If on restart robot does not start, another Robot Athlete continues
