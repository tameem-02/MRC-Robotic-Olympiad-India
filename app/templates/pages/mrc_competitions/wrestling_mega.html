üéØ GOAL

The aim of Robot Athletes is to knock out the opponent on the specially designed wrestling ring and be declared the winner.

Wrestling does not require damage or destruction of opponent, but only displacement outside the ring.

‚ö†Ô∏è Robot Athletes violating core values will be disqualified.

1. Team Formation

  • 1.2 Team Size: 2-3 members per team
  • 1.3 Chief Coach: One member as Chief Coach responsible for communication, technical inspection, and robot handling

‚öôÔ∏è ROBOT ATHLETE REQUIREMENTS

Competition for Robot Athletes regardless of robotic kit or hardware. All compete together in each age division.

2.1. GENERAL SPECIFICATIONS

  1. Any design allowed (if not restricted by 2.2)
  2. Dimensions: 20cm √ó 20cm (200mm). No height limit

üèÜ COMPETITION PROCEDURE

4. Format

  1. Bout consists of 3 rounds, total time 3 minutes (max 1 min extension)
  2. Overtime: First point wins, or judges decide
  3. If one robot doesn't start: restart. Still doesn't start = other wins

4.1. Course of Competition

  1. Robots divided into groups. Group/quarter/final system
  2. Random matching by lottery after official start
  3. 2 rescheduling breaks (5 min each) per team
  4. ‚ö†Ô∏è DON'T BE LATE! If not at field in 5 min = DISQUALIFIED!
  5. 2 coaches per team in waiting/playing area
  6. ü•Ω Mandatory: Gloves and safety glasses. Missing = disqualification
  7. Max 1 min break between rounds for cleaning
  8. No touching robot after placement until referee signals

üîç 4.2. ROBOT REQUIREMENTS CHECK

  1. Check dimensions: 20.3√ó20.3cm box/frame without bottom. No height limit
  2. Weigh on digital scale. Max 3005g
  3. Check protective equipment
  4. Place unique number on outer casing
  5. Demonstrate program to committee
  6. Robot not restricted after approval. Can test/program without changing dimensions
  7. Photo taken of each robot with visible number

üéÆ 4.3. MATCH FLOW

1. Robot Placement

  • Teams place robots simultaneously after referee signal
  • Robots placed facing each other, 50cm distance
  • After placement, coaches retreat to safe area

2. Beginning

  • Referee checks start-stop modules
  • Counts 3-2-1 and presses start
  • Launch after trainers in safe area

5. Race Time

  • Duration: 4 minutes total
  • Extension: Max 1 minute if requested

üìä 4.4 SCORING

A point is given when:

  1. Team legally forces opponent's body to touch area outside ring
  2. Opposing robot touches space outside ring by itself
  3. Opposing robot completely left field, even if other touched outside first (after contact)
  4. Opposing robot damaged/unable to continue (coach announces)
  5. Both leave field simultaneously: robot being pushed out loses (even if pusher touches outside first)

Judge Decision Criteria:

  1. Technical advantages in movement/operation
  2. Penalty points during match
  3. Attitude of players

üèÜ 4.5. WINNER DETERMINATION

  1. Robot with most points wins
  2. In case of knockout
  3. Tie: referee awards victory based on tactics, aggression, activity
  4. No points: judge may decide no winner

üîÑ 4.6. REPLAY CONDITIONS

  1. Robot doesn't start: Restart. Still doesn't = other wins
  2. Confused/wandering (10s): Restart. Repeats = most active wins
  3. Fast stuck on slow (5s): Restart. Repeats = faster attacker wins
  4. Two fast stuck (5s): Restart. Repeats = fastest attacker wins
  5. Robot has 1 pt, next no winner: Only 2 rematches. No winner = 1-pt robot wins match
  6. Both stop (5s): First stopper declared unmotivated. Opponent gets point
  7. Both touch outside simultaneously: Can't determine first = rematch

üõ†Ô∏è 4.7. REPAIRS & MODIFICATIONS

From approval until match over (all rounds):

  • ‚ùå No repairs during match
  • ‚ùå No battery change/charge during match
  • ‚ùå No blade change during match
  • ‚ùå No reprogramming during match
  • ‚ùå Detached blade: must continue without it
  • ‚úÖ Max 1 min break between rounds for cleaning/configuration
  • ‚úÖ Battery/repairs/replacements after match ends
  • If robot breaks and can't continue = opponent wins

‚ö†Ô∏è 4.8-4.9. VIOLATIONS & OFFENSES

Major Violations:

  • Insults to opponent/judges
  • Missing protective equipment
  • Not retreating to safe area

Minor Offenses (Warning):

  1. Enter ring during match (unless referee stopped)
  2. Demand stop without reason
  3. Against referee judgment
  4. Actions contrary to fair-play
  5. Leave waiting area without notifying
  6. 2 warnings = point to opponent OR disqualification

‚öñÔ∏è 5. OBJECTIONS & FLEXIBILITY

  • No objections against judges' decisions
  • Coaches may submit objections to Committee before competition end if doubts about rules
  • Rules flexible enough to accommodate changes if published prior and maintained consistently
